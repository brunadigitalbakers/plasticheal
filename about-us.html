About

PLASTICHEAL project aims at providing new methodologies and evidence to regulators by combining the use of breakthrough research and reliable test methods to set the knowledge basis for adequate risk assessment of MNPLs.

The main objectives of PLASTICHEAL are

To provide legislators with data, methodologies, experimental models and biological endpoints of effect for risk assessment and decision-making, by filling current knowledge gaps in terms of MNPLs hazard to human health. Furthermore, to perform a pilot exposure and risk assessment for a selected number of MNPLs highly present in the environment.

To develop new more comprehensive methodologies, experimental models and biological endpoints of effect able to provide a more solid understanding of the complex interplay between MNPLs modes of action and adverse outcome pathways in humans. This is aimed at supporting regulatory decision-making and have a potential to take on a more prominent role in future frameworks.

Specific objectives

  • To establish a continuous dialog with stakeholders and regulators.
  • To prepare a set of reference materials to be used by the consortium for MNPLs safety assessment, along with an optimized dispersion protocol.
  • To develop and adapt existing analytical methods for detection, identification and quantification of MNPLs in environmental and biological matrices.
  • To determine MNPLs occurrence in air, water and food sources.
  • To monitor MNPLs occurrence in biological samples from individuals occupationally exposed to MNPLs.
  • To determine MNPLs effects in biological samples from individuals occupationally exposed to MNPLs.
  • To determine the influence of MNPLs structure in the potential hazard.
  • To determine MNPLs-induced alterations on the human gastrointestinal microbiota.
  • To adapt complex systems of in vitro RT barrier and GIT barrier (including in vitro digestion) to the MNPLs field.
  • To study the toxicokinetic and toxicodynamic of MNPLs following exposure of in vivo models via the pulmonary and gastrointestinal routes.
  • To develop an ex vivo blood model to explore the MNPLs effects in immune blood cells.
  • To study the action of MNPLs in immune system function.
  • To assess long-term MNPLs effects by using epithelial and stem-cell based in vitro models.
  • To integrate data for PBPK modelling and bioinformatics/ computational analysis.
  • To integrate the results to generate a framework for risk assessment.
  • To analyse the influence of gender dimension on the MNPLs- associated effects.

CUSP

PLASTICHEAL, together with AURORA , IMPTOX , PlasticsFatE , and POLYRISK, is one of the five European main research projects currently working to understand micro- and nanoplastics (MNPLs) in our environment and their exposures and impacts on health. These projects are publicly funded under a call within the European Union's Horizon 2020 program. To optimise synergies, the five projects have formed the European Research Cluster to Understand the Health Impacts of Micro- and Nanoplastic (CUSP). The results of the researches will contribute to the health-relevant aims of the European Strategy for Plastics in a Circular Economy and the Bioeconomy Strategy, as well as the REACH restrictions on intentionally added MNPLs to products, by providing new evidence for better preventive policies.
